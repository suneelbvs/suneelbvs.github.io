# Toxicity Forecasts: Data-Driven AI/ML Models

A book chapter on **"Toxicity Forecasts: Navigating Data-Driven AI/ML Models —
From Theory to Practice"**, published by Apple Academic Press
(Taylor & Francis Group) in *Artificial Intelligence for Chemical Sciences:
Concepts, Models and Applications*.

![AI/ML models for toxicity forecasts](/images/AI_ML%20Models_for_Toxicity_Forecasts.jpg)

## What it covers

The chapter focuses on the **practical implementation** of AI/ML with toxicity
datasets, with hands-on coding snippets and detailed explanations using:

- :material-language-python: **RDKit** for cheminformatics and featurization
- :material-flask: **DeepChem** for deep-learning models
- :material-chart-scatter-plot: **scikit-learn** for classical ML baselines

It bridges the gap between theory and reproducible, working pipelines — the kind
a practitioner can actually run on a real toxicity dataset.
